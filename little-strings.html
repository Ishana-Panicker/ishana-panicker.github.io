Overview

During Little Strings Summer Camp, I worked as a violin instructor for beginner students. I helped students learn foundational violin techniques in a supportive group setting. Here I will detail what I taught in the camp, an example of a day in the camp, additional info, pictures, parents reviews, and more!

A Day in Little Strings

Today is the first day of camp! Today, we'll be introducing you to the violin and getting you familiar with it. Firstly, we'll start by introducing ourselves and doing some icebreakers. Then we'll get our violins out! First, we need to learn the parts of the violin. We'll do a fun activity to learn them. Then, we need to learn strings names! This is very important. We'll do another activity to learn these. After everyone's got the parts of the violin and the string names down, we'll move on to how to hold the violin. We'll do a fun competition to help us build endurance. Once everyone has mastered how to hold the violin, we can move on. Now, we'll take a short break and do a fun game as we relax! Once the break is over, we can move to pizzicato. That's a special technique you learn even before you use your bow! Once we learn about pizzicato, we can end the day off by learning a full song that you can play at home! We'll distrubute the overview and homework, and that concludes our first day of camp!
